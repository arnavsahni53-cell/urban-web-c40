Urban Web

The open layer that
connects how a city moves.

The web connected information. GPS connected position. Mobile networks connected devices. Urban Web connects how a city moves.

A shared infrastructure.
Owned by no one.

It is a shared standard that any mobility service can adopt once, making it reachable by every other service on the network.

Not a platform, not an intermediary — a shared standard. Like HTTP for the web, but for how a city moves.

Cities that build on this standard don't just move people more efficiently. They create the conditions for culture, climate action, economic opportunity, and ideas that haven't yet been imagined to find each other.

Four ideas.
One fundamental shift.

Every network that changed the world worked this way.

01
Join once.

A single connection to Urban Web makes any mobility service immediately reachable by every other service on the network. It connects to a shared layer, not individual partners.

02
Reach everything.

New services become available across the ecosystem the moment they join.

03
Grow beyond mobility.

As more services join, the city’s mobility layer becomes part of a broader network of commerce, logistics, energy and more.

04
Nothing in the middle.

Urban Web defines how services talk. Like TCP/IP, it sits beneath every connection it enables.

Not just what you build today.
What others build tomorrow.

The value of open infrastructure is not what you build on it. It's what others build, without asking permission.

01
The obvious unlock

When every mobility service in a city speaks the same standard, any two of them can work together without a bilateral agreement, a custom build, or a procurement cycle.

02
The deeper point

The more important aspect is what nobody planned. When everything sits on a shared open layer, the conditions exist for solutions that nobody has thought of yet, including the next generation of AI-native interfaces that discover, transact, and coordinate across a city.

03
For the city

Cities gain a real-time view of how their mobility network is actually being used. Not service by service, but across the whole. Policy, investment, and infrastructure decisions made with data that has never existed before.

04
For the future

New mobility categories like autonomous vehicles, urban aviation, and energy-as-transport join the same layer without rebuilding the connections. The infrastructure grows with the city, not behind it.

Networks for Humanity (NFH) is a global non-profit initiative building open, interoperable and decentralised digital infrastructure. Their shared network powers Urban Web, providing the open standard that any mobility service can connect to.

WRI is a global research organisation working to improve people's lives, protect nature and stabilise the climate. They bring city relationships, policy expertise, and on-the-ground research that makes Urban Web work in practice.
